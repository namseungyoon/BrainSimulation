TITLE TEXT: Measuring the LFP across the whole network (point sources)
SUBTITLE TEXT: Every neuron is a point current source; the field is their distance-weighted sum

Panel A, left: Point-source field model
Equation (volume conductor, homogeneous isotropic medium):
LFP(x, t) = 1/(4*pi*sigma) * sum over i of  I_i(t) / |x - r_i|
- I_i = the net synaptic/transmembrane current of neuron i (here: sum of its port currents g_p(V-E_p)).
- r_i = position of neuron i (soma point); x = electrode position.
- sigma = extracellular conductivity = 1/rho, rho = 333 ohm*cm.
In code this is LFP = - sum_i w_i I_i with w_i = S*rho/(4*pi*d_i), d_i = |x - r_i|.

Panel B, center: Current sub-sampling (what we actually record)
- We record 128 x-stratified pyramidal somata (of ~89,678 pyramidal cells at full scale) inside one electrode ROI at (200, 100, 120) um.
- Why subsample: each recorded cell x variable is a GPU->host copy every sample; the full population would be far more transfer.
- The proxy therefore ESTIMATES the field from a spatial sample, not the whole population.

Panel C, right: Extending to the full network
- Whole-population field: sum over ALL pyramidal cells, not 128.
- LAMINAR profile: several electrodes along depth (stratum oriens / pyramidale / radiatum / lacunosum) to capture the dipole, not one point.
- Convergence check: recompute LFP with 128 / 256 / 512 / 1024 sampled cells and multiple electrode positions; a real signal is stable under sampling.
Draw a pyramidal-cell layer with a vertical multi-contact electrode spanning SO/SP/SR/SLM.

Panel D, bottom strip: Caveats
Point sources at the soma approximate a cell whose current is really distributed along its morphology (line sources). Interneurons are excluded from the proxy. Final evidence needs sample-size and electrode-position convergence plus comparison to a pyramidal spike-density signal.
