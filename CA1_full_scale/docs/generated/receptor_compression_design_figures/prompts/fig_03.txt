TITLE TEXT: Why metrics beyond reconstruction are needed
SUBTITLE TEXT: A waveform can be recoverable but still be the wrong runtime decision

MAIN VISUAL:
Five horizontal lanes from left to right:
1. Reconstruction fidelity: can the 20-port approximation match the original kernel waveform?
2. Utility weighting: does the mismatch matter for network current at V = -55 mV?
3. Target priority: is the affected postsynaptic cell important for theta and stability?
4. Runtime efficiency: does each synapse still route to one receptor port?
5. Safety/provenance: can the exact assignment be audited and rejected loudly if context is wrong?

It answers: "Could two selected ports approximate this kernel?"

RIGHT CALLOUT:
Hard medoids answer the deployment question.

BOTTOM CONTRAST TABLE:
Metric | Answers | Why needed

BOTTOM DECISION LINE:
The chosen runtime strategy must satisfy all five lanes, not just the first lane.
